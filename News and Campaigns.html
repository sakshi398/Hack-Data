Mumbaikars and celebs campaign for organ donation awareness

Every year over five lakh Indians lose their lives waiting for an organ. According to the 2017 data, over 800 organ donations have taken place which represents only 0.8 people per million individuals donated organs. In order to address this concern, the team at Fortis, Mumbai hosted the #MoreToGive initiative in Kurla yesterday. In its fourth year now, the #MoreToGive campaign focused on raising awareness and drawing in organ donation pledges. Padmashree awardee, Dhanraj Pillay former captain of the Indian national hockey team, actress Richa Chadda, adguru Pralhad Kakkar supported the initiative along with the medical and non-medical teams from the chain of hospitals.

Tamil Nadu receives best performance award for organ donation donation for fourth time.

CHENNAI: The Tamil Nadu health department on Tuesday received Union ministry of health and family welfare's best performance award for organ donation, for the fourth time in a row. State health minister C Vijaya Baskar received the award for the department at the ninth annual National Organ Day held in Delhi. Tamil Nadu has one of the largest organ transplant programmes in the country that has retrieved organs from more than 10,000 patients. The state brought in rules for organ donation and transplant after a series of organtheft cases, including from those among victims of tsunami.

Vice-President for stepping up campaign on organ donation.

HYDERABAD: Vice-President M Venkaiah Naidu Friday called for stepping up the campaign for organ donation to meet the huge demand in view of rising incidence of kidney, liver, heart and other organ failures.
